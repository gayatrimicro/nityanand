Special Features

Kunds

1. Kunds

These are bathing pools of hot water close to Samadhi temple. Perennial hot water spring fills the pools. Bhagwan Nityanand loved to bathe in the kunds and so do the devotees .Ailments get cured by bathing in the kunds. Sanstha maintains the kunds and changing rooms which have been newly renovated. The kunds are open from 3.00 am to 10 pm on all days.

Kailash Niwas

2. Kailash Niwas

It is on the main road and south west of Samadhi temple. This was the heart of pilgrimage of devotees when Bhagwan physically lived there. It contains priceless sculptures and Bhagwan's photographs. Places where Bhagwan sat, slept and moved around are maintained with great reverence. In the peaceful feeling induced by Bhagwan's residence -most devotees sit down to meditate. Some books written about Bhagwan Nityanand and Chidaksha Gita - The only record of Bhagwan's sayings are kept/sold in Kailash Niwas.

Nirvan Sthaan

3. Nirvan Sthaan

Bhagwan Nityanand shifted to the big Dharmashala called Banglorewala building staying there for nearly 2 weeks before taking Mahasamadhi on 8th Aug 1961.The Hall and rooms sanctified by his stay are maintained as a temple and daily poojas performed there .The building made by a prominent devotee from Bangalore is to the north of Kailash Niwas

Balbhojan

4. Balbhojan / Annapurna Canteen

Bhagwan Nityanand loved to play amongst the children and feed them. Balbhojan was very dear to Bhagwan. This is one of the main activity of the Sanstha. Hundreds of local children from and around Ganeshpuri are given free wholesome food everyday. The children joyfully chant OM NAMO BHAGWATE NITYANANDAYA as soon as they enter the Hall they sit down and are served, and all start eating together. A large number of Bhandaras are also organized here after the children have had their meal. Balbhojan is to north of Kailash Niwas.

Annapurna is to north of Balbhojan. The sanstha employees organize cleaning, cooking and serving wholesome meals to visiting devotees. The timings and current rates are as follows:- Nasta (breakfast)- 6 a.m. to 10 a.m for Rs.10, Lunch -11 a.m. to 2 p.m. for Rs.20, Dinner -7 p.m to 9 p.m. for Rs.20, Sadhu coupons Rs.20 (nashta + tea) and Rs. 15 for meals, coffee for Rs. 7 and Dahi for Rs. 4.

Krishna Temple

5. Temples

Krishna Temple - Established by Bhagwan Nityanand himself. Now just behind His Samadhi Shrine.
Bhadrakali Temple - Near the ST bus stand on the main road. The temple was established by Bhagwan Nityanand to honor Shiva's consort.
Navgrah Temple - South of Samadhi temple and adjacent to it. Yagnas are held here.The important one being on punyatithi day & vasant panchmi.

Basic Facilities

6. Basic Facilities

Parking Area - The parking area is to the east of Samadhi shrine and entry to it is from Kailash Niwas. Sanstha maintains the parking area and also grow trees around periphery for greenery.
Toilet - Clean and adequate number of toilet for gents and ladies are to the east of parking area. Sanstha is looking after the toilets as well as area around them.
Dispensary - There is a basic dispensary run by PRASAD CHIKITSA where primary health care can be taken care of. Also there is a govt. run primary health hospital at Vajreshwari.
Six Buildings are maintained by the Sanstha to provide accommodation for visiting devotees. The charges vary for different type of room/facilities.
